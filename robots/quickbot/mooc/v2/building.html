1. Rocker Switch Wiring Assembly

Number to build: 1

Parts

  1. 2 - 2-pin Female Red JST Battery Connector
  2. 1 - 2-pin Female 2mm-pitch JST Uh-oh Connector
  3. 1 - 3-pin Rocker Switch
  4. 3 - 1/2” Heat Shrink Tubing

Rocker Switch Wiring Parts


Tools

  1. Wire Strippers
  2. Wire Cutters
  3. Soldering Iron

Instructions

  1. Cut battery connector wires to 3.5” and strip 1/4” from ends.
  2. Twist stripped ends of black wires of battery connector together and twist stripped ends of red wires of battery connector together.
  3. Insert black wires through one heat shrink tube and insert red wries through another one of the heat shrink tubes.
  4. Untwist Uh-oh wire 1” and strip 1/4” from ends.
  5. Insert red wire of Uh-oh connector through the last heat shrink tube.
  6. Solder red wires of battery connectors to the pin on the “O” side of switch.
    • Pro tip: Solder wires together first, then solder to the pin of the switch.
  7. Solder red wire of Uh-oh connector to the middle pin of the switch.
  8. Solder all black wires together.

  9. Heat shrink the tubing around solder points.

    Rocker Switch Wiring Assembly

3. Voltage Cable Assembly Set

Number to build: 1 (One 9V and One 5V cable)

Parts

  1. 2 - 2-pin Female 0.1in-pitch JST Volt Reg Connector
  2. 1 - 1x2-pin 0.1” Crimp Connector Housing
  3. 1 - 1x1-pin 0.1” Crimp Connector Housing
  4. 4 - 0.1” Male Crimp Pins

Voltage Cable Parts


Tools

  1. Wire Strippers
  2. Wire Cutters
  3. Crimpers
  4. Needle Nose Pliers

Instructions

  1. Cut one set of wires to 5.5” and strip 1/4” from ends.
  2. Cut the other set of wires to 4.5” and 1/4” from ends.
  3. Crimp on crimp pins to each wire end.
  4. Insert 5.5” wires into two 1x1-pin connector housing.
  5. Insert 4.5” wires into one 1x2-pin connector housing.
    • Note: The 5.5” cable will be for the 9V regulator and the 4.5” cable will be for the 5V regulator.

Voltage Cable Assembly Set


4. Encoder Cable Assembly

Number to build: 2

(1 with purple/gray ribbon and 1 with red/orange ribbon)

Parts

  1. 1 - Encoder Ribbon Cable
  2. 5 - Female Molex Crimp Pins
  3. 1 - 5-pin Molex Header
  4. 2 - 1x2-pin 0.1” Crimp Connector Housing
  5. 1 - 1x1-pin 0.1” Crimp Connector Housing
  6. 5 - 0.1” Male Crimp Pins

Encoder Cable Parts


Tools

  1. Wire Strippers
  2. Wire Cutters
  3. Crimpers
  4. Needle Nose Pliers

Instructions

  1. Separate 3/4” of the ribbon cable in to individual wires.
  2. Strip 1/8” of insulation from each wire and twist ends so they are not frayed.
  3. Crimp female Molex crimp pin to each wire.
    • Note: Make sure to crimp pin in correct orientation.


  4. Separate 3/4” of the other end of the ribbon cable into individual wires.
  5. Strip 1/8” of insulation from each wire and twist ends so they are not frayed.
  6. Crimp 0.1” male crimp pin to each wire.
  7. Insert white & gray or yellow & orange wires into 1x2 0.1” crimp connector housing.
  8. Insert black & blue or green & brown wires into 1x2 0.1” crimp connector housing.

  9. Insert purple or red into 1x1 0.1” crimp connector housing.

    Encoder Cable Assembly

14. Bottom Chassis With Motors Assembly

Number to build: 1

Parts

  1. 2 - Motor-Encoder Assembly
  2. 1 - Bottom Chassis Plate
  3. 4 - Motor Holders (2 modified, 2 normal)
  4. 4 - M3*30 Screw
  5. 4 - M3 Nut

Bottom Chassis With Motors Parts


Tools

  1. Drill
  2. Dremel
  3. Small Screw Driver

Instructions

  1. Drill four holes towards the front of the bottom chassis plate as shown in the picture. These holes are for mounting the voltage regulators, so use the voltage regulators as a guild for the placement of the holes.

    Voltage Regulator Holes on the Topside Bottom Chassis


  2. Modify the motor holders with the dremel as shown in the picture. This allows for the encoder to fit on the motor with the motor holders, so use the motor-encoder assembly as a guild when doing this modification.

    Modified Motor Holders



  3. Place modified motor holders through slots in the bottom chassis plate, orientated such that modified edge of motor holders are on the front side of chassis (rounded edge). The mounts should stick out towards the bottom of the bottom chassis.


  4. Install motor-encoder assembly with the other unmodified motor holder, two M3*30 screws, and two M3 nuts per motor with black wires of motors away from chassis.

    Bottom Chassis With Motors Assembly

18. Bottom Chassis With Wiring Assembly #1

Number to build: 1

Parts

  1. 1 - Bottom Chassis With IR Sensors Assembly
  2. 2 - Encoder Cable Assembly
  3. 1 - Voltage Cable Assembly Set

Bottom Chassis With Wiring #1 Parts


Instructions

  1. Plug shorter voltage cable into the “OUT” of the right voltage regulator (5V) and the red wire into front “+” rail and the black wire into front “-“ rail of the breadboard.

    5V Red = 5V Rail, 5V Black = GND Rail


  2. Plug longer voltage cable into the “OUT” of the left voltage regulator (9V) and the red wire into pin 8 of H-bridge and the black wire into pin 5 of the H-bridge.

    9V Red = Pin 8, 9V Black = Pin 5


  3. Plug in blue/purple encoder cable ribbon into right encoder, making sure the black wire goes to “-“ pin of the encoder.

  4. Plug in brown/red encoder cable ribbon into left encoder, making sure the green wire goes to “-“ pin of the encoder.

    Encoder Wiring


  5. String encoder ribbons through middle slot of chassis and plug into breadboard according to the following:

    • Blue = 5V Rail
    • Purple = GND Rail
    • Gray = Pin 9 Logic Level Converter
    • White = Pin 8 Logic Level Converter
    • Black = GND Rail
    • Brown = 5V Rail
    • Red = GND Rail
    • Orange = Pin 11 Logic Level Converter
    • Yellow = Pin 10 Logic Level Converter

    • Green = GND Rail

    • Note: If you are using the SparkFun logic level converter the only difference to the AdaFruit logic level converter is that the high, low, and ground connections are on the middle pins instead of the outside pins. Just adjust the wiring accordingly.

    Encoder Wiring


  6. Plug in left motor black wire into pin 3 of the H-bridge, plug left motor red wire into pin 6 of the H-bridge.

  7. Plug in right motor black wire into pin 11 of the H-bridge, plug right motor red wire into pin 14 of the H-bridge.

    Motor Wiring

24. QuickBot Assembly

Number to build: 1

Parts

  1. 1 - Bottom Chassis With Wiring Assembly #2
  2. 1 - Top Chassis Plate With IR Wiring Assembly
  3. 1 - BBB-Encoder Wiring Assembly
  4. 1 - BBB-IR Wiring Assembly
  5. 1 - BBB-Power Wiring Assembly
  6. 1 - BBB-Motor Wiring Assembly
  7. 4 - M3 Screw

QuickBot Assembly Parts


Tools

  1. Small Screw Driver

Instructions

BBB Pin Out


Wiring Diagram


  1. Connect green-yellow BBB-Encoder wiring assembly to the logic level converter (LLC) and to the BBB.
    • Green wire = LLC pin 2 <-> BBB pin P9_22.
    • Yellow wire = LLC pin 3 <-> BBB pin P9_21.
  2. Connect blue-white BBB-Encoder wiring assembly to the logic level converter (LLC) and to the BBB.
    • Blue wire = LLC pin 4 <-> BBB pin P9_26.
    • White wire = LLC pin 5 <-> BBB pin P9_24.
  3. Connect BBB-IR wiring assembly to the 10K resistors and to the BBB.
    • Yellow wire = left IR <-> BBB pin P9_40.
    • Green wire = left front IR <-> BBB pin P9_48.
    • Blue wire = front IR <-> BBB pin P9_36.
    • White wire = right front <-> BBB pin P9_35.
    • Red wire = right <-> BBB pin P9_33.
  4. Connect BBB-Power wiring assembly to the power rails and to the BBB.
    • Black wire = GND rail <-> BBB pin P9_1.
    • White wire = 3.3V rail <-> BBB pin P9_3.
    • Red wire = 5V rail <-> BBB pin P9_5.
  5. Connect yellow-red-green BBB-Motor wiring assembly to the H-Bridge and to the BBB.
    • Yellow wire = H-Bridge pin 1 <-> BBB pin P9_16
    • Red wire = H-Bridge pin 2 <-> BBB pin P8_16.
    • Green wire = H-Bridge pin 7 <-> BBB pin P8_14
  6. Connect white-black-blue BBB-Motor wiring assembly to the H-Bridge and to the BBB.
    • White wire = H-Bridge pin 15 <-> BBB pin P8_10.
    • Black wire = H-Bridge pin 10 <-> BBB pin P8_12.
    • Blue wire = H-Bridge pin 9 <-> BBB pin P9_14.


  7. Plug in the 2-pin female 0.1in-pitch JST volt reg connectors to the voltage regulator “IN” connection.
  8. Plug the left IR sensor white wire into the 20K resistor all the way to the left.
  9. Plug the front IR sensor white wire into the middle 20K resistor.
  10. Plug the right IR sensor white wire into the 20K resistor all the way to the right.
  11. Plug the IR sensor red wires into the 5V rail.
  12. Plug the IR sensor black wires into the GND rail.

  13. Attach top plate assembly to bottom plate assembly using four screws.
