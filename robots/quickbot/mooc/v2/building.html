1. Rocker Switch Wiring Assembly

Number to build: 1

Parts

  1. 2 - 2-pin Female Red JST Battery Connector
  2. 1 - 2-pin Female 2mm-pitch JST Uh-oh Connector
  3. 1 - 3-pin Rocker Switch
  4. 3 - 1/2” Heat Shrink Tubing

Rocker Switch Wiring Parts


Tools

  1. Wire Strippers
  2. Wire Cutters
  3. Soldering Iron

Instructions

  1. Cut battery connector wires to 3.5” and strip 1/4” from ends.
  2. Twist stripped ends of black wires of battery connector together and twist stripped ends of red wires of battery connector together.
  3. Insert black wires through one heat shrink tube and insert red wries through another one of the heat shrink tubes.
  4. Untwist Uh-oh wire 1” and strip 1/4” from ends.
  5. Insert red wire of Uh-oh connector through the last heat shrink tube.
  6. Solder red wires of battery connectors to the pin on the “O” side of switch.
    • Pro tip: Solder wires together first, then solder to the pin of the switch.
  7. Solder red wire of Uh-oh connector to the middle pin of the switch.
  8. Solder all black wires together.

  9. Heat shrink the tubing around solder points.

    Rocker Switch Wiring Assembly

3. Encoder Cable Assembly

Number to build: 2

(1 with purple/gray ribbon and 1 with red/orange ribbon)

Parts

  1. 1 - Encoder Ribbon Cable
  2. 5 - Female Molex Crimp Pins
  3. 1 - 5-pin Molex Header
  4. 2 - 1x2-pin 0.1” Crimp Connector Housing
  5. 1 - 1x1-pin 0.1” Crimp Connector Housing
  6. 5 - 0.1” Male Crimp Pins

Encoder Cable Parts


Tools

  1. Wire Strippers
  2. Wire Cutters
  3. Crimpers
  4. Needle Nose Pliers

Instructions

  1. Separate 3/4” of the ribbon cable in to individual wires.
  2. Strip 1/8” of insulation from each wire and twist ends so they are not frayed.
  3. Crimp female Molex crimp pin to each wire.
    • Note: Make sure to crimp pin in correct orientation.**


  4. Separate 3/4” of the other end of the ribbon cable into individual wires.
  5. Strip 1/8” of insulation from each wire and twist ends so they are not frayed.
  6. Crimp 0.1” male crimp pin to each wire.
  7. Insert white & gray or yellow & orange wires into 1x2 0.1” crimp connector housing.
  8. Insert black & blue or green & brown wire into 1x2 0.1” crimp connector housing.

  9. Insert purple or red into 1x1 0.1” crimp connector housing.

    Encoder Cable Assembly


4. IR Cable Assembly

Number to build: 5

Parts

  1. 1 - 3-pin Female 2mm-pitch JST IR Sensor Connector
  2. 1 - 1x2-pin 0.1” Crimp Connector Housing
  3. 1 - 1x1-pin 0.1” Crimp Connector Housing
  4. 3 - 0.1” Male Crimp Pins

IR Cable Parts


Tools

  1. Wire Strippers
  2. Wire Cutters
  3. Crimpers
  4. Needle Nose Pliers

Instructions

  1. Cut connector wires to 7” and strip off 1/8” of insulation from the ends.
  2. Crimp on crimp pins to each wire end.
  3. Insert red and black wires into 1x2-pin connector housing.

  4. Insert white wire into 1x1-pin connector housing.

    IR Cable Assembly

8. Motor-Encoder Assembly

Number to build: 2

Parts

  1. 1 - Encoder Disc Sticker
  2. 1 - Speed Board Disc
  3. 1 - Wheel Encoder
  4. 1 - Motor

Breadboard Parts


Tools

  1. Drill
  2. 9/64 Drill Bit

Instructions

  1. Stick encoder disc sticker onto wheel encoder disc.
    • Note: Make sure to place directly in the center of the disc!

  2. Enlarge wheel encoder mounting holes using approximately a 9/64 drill bit.

    Encoder Holes to Enlarge


  3. Fit wheel encoder onto motor using round notch as an alignment guide.

  4. Slide disc with sticker on to motor axle with the sticker facing the encoder. Keep disc flush with the end of the motor axle.

    Motor-Encoder Assembly


9. Bottom Chassis With Motors Assembly

Number to build: 1

Parts

  1. 2 - Motor-Encoder Assembly
  2. 1 - Bottom Chassis Plate
  3. 4 - Motor Holders (2 modified, 2 normal)
  4. 4 - M3*30 Screw
  5. 4 - M3 Nut

Bottom Chassis With Motors Parts


Tools

  1. Drill
  2. Dremel
  3. Small Screw Driver

Instructions

  1. Drill four holes towards the front of the bottom chassis plate as shown in the picture. These holes are for mounting the voltage regulators, so use the voltage regulators as a guild for the placement of the holes.

    Voltage Regulator Holes on the Topside Bottom Chassis


  2. Modify the motor holders as shown in the picture. This allows for the encoder to fit with the motor holders, so use the motor-encoder assembly as a guild.

    Modified Motor Holders



  3. Place modified motor holders through slots in the bottom chassis plate, orientated such that modified edge of motor holders are on the front side of chassis (rounded edge). The mounts should stick out towards the bottom of the bottom chassis.


  4. Install motor-encoder assembly with the other unmodified motor holder, two M3*30 screws, and two M3 nuts per motor with black wires of motors away from chassis.

    Bottom Chassis With Motors Assembly
